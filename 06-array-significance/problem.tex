	\section*{Prompt}
	Pranav and Prithvi from CodeJam 22 are back, and are now working on a mathematical proof. They have determined the \textit{significance factor function} $s(x[])$ of an array to be as follows:
	
	$$\left[\frac{\text{number of primes in }x[]}{\text{highest common factor of the composite numbers in }x[]}\cdot \text{length of }x[]\right]$$ 
	\textit{square brackets indicate Greatest Integer Function}
	
	When there are no primes in $x[]$ or $x[]$ has no composite elements, $s(x[]) = 0$.
	
	Given a list $n[]$, determine the significance factor of the array.

	\subsection*{Input Format}
	The first and only line of input contains a space-separated list $n[]$

	\subsection*{Output Format}
	Your output must have a single line containing $s(n[])$.

	\subsection*{Constraints}
	$1 \leq \text{length of }n[] \leq 10^5$ and $1 \leq n[i] \leq 100$
